# Market Psychology

## Overview

Markets are made of humans, and humans are driven by psychology. Understanding the collective mental state of market participants gives you an edge — it's not just numbers on a chart.

## Market Sentiment Cycle

### Bull Phase
```
Optimism → Excitement → Thrill → Euphoria
```
Everyone's buying, FOMO everywhere, valuations stretch. Time to think about [[risk-management]].

### Bear Phase
```
Anxiety → Denial → Fear → Desperation → Panic
```
Selling pressure accelerates, bad news dominates. Opportunities for [[mean-reversion]] strategies.

## Key Psychological Patterns

### Herd Mentality

**Fear Of Missing Out (FOMO):** Buying because everyone else is buying. Usually happens near tops.

**Loss Aversion:** Selling winners too early, holding losers too long. Humans feel losses 2x more than gains of equal size.

### Confirmation Bias

Seeking information that confirms your bias, ignoring contradictory evidence. This leads to [[forced-engagement-produces-weak-connections]] — forcing trades that fit your narrative rather than following the market.

## Emotional Control Techniques

### Pre-Trade Checklist

Before entering any trade, ask:
1. What's my [[risk-reward-ratio]]?
2. Am I trading my plan or my emotions?
3. What would make this trade invalid?

### Post-Trade Review

- Did I follow my [[trading-routine]]?
- What emotions did I feel during the trade?
- What can I learn from the outcome?

See [[emotional-control]] for deeper techniques.

## Market Psychology Indicators

### Put/Call Ratio
- High (>1.0) → Excessive fear → Potential bullish reversal
- Low (<0.5) → Excessive greed → Potential bearish reversal

### VIX (Fear Index)
- High (>30) → High fear → Buying opportunity for contrarians
- Low (<15) → Low fear → Caution for long positions

### Social Media Sentiment
When everyone's talking about the same trade, it's usually late. See [[fear-and-greed]].

## Integration

Market psychology connects to:

- [[risk-management]] — Fear prevents proper risk-taking, greed causes over-risking
- [[momentum-trading]] — Greed drives momentum, fear kills it
- [[breakout-trading]] — FOMO fuel breakouts, lack of conviction causes fakeouts

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Revenge trading** — Trying to recover losses quickly → See [[emotional-control]]
2. **Trading boredom** — Taking trades just to be in the market → [[forced-engagement-produces-weak-connections]]
3. **Overconfidence** — After a winning streak → Reduce [[position-sizing]], increase humility

## Quick Mental Check

If you find yourself thinking:
- "I can't miss this move!" → You're likely late
- "I have to make my money back!" → You're emotional, stop trading
- "Everyone else is wrong, I'm right" → Dangerous territory

Time for your [[trading-routine]].
