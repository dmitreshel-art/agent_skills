# Breakout Trading

## Overview

Breakout trading catches moves when price breaks out of consolidation. The market has absorbed all supply at a level, now breaks through. Greed often fuels the move — maintain [[emotional-control]].

## Core Principle

**Price breaking out of range often continues in breakout direction.**

Sometimes it's a fakeout.

## Key Elements

### Consolidation

- Range-bound price action
- Decreasing volatility
- Volume contracting
- Building tension

### Breakout Signal

- Price breaks above/below consolidation
- Volume spike (confirmation)
- Holds beyond level
- Follow-through

### Fakeout Risk

- Breakout then reversal
- Trap participants
- Quick stop required

## Setup

### 1. Identify Consolidation

- Clear range defined
- Multiple touches of levels
- Volume contracting
- Time: At least 20 bars

### 2. Wait for Breakout

- Break above resistance (long)
- Break below support (short)
- Volume spike confirms
- Price holds beyond level

### 3. Enter and Manage

**Entry:** On breakout pullback or immediate
**Stop:** Opposite side of consolidation
**Target:** Measured move (height of range)
**R:R:** 2:1 to 3:1

See [[stop-loss-strategies]] for placement.

## Timeframe Alignment

Trade breakouts on higher timeframes:
- Daily/4-hour breakouts more reliable
- 1-hour breakouts more frequent, more false
- Multi-timeframe confirmation improves odds

## Common Mistakes

- Chasing extended moves (not true breakout)
- Entering before breakout (anticipation)
- Too tight stops (stopped on noise)
- Ignoring volume (low volume = suspect)

See [[fear-and-greed]] on FOMO driving bad entries.
