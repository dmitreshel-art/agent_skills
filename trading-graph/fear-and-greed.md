# Fear and Greed

## Overview

Fear and greed are the two primary emotions that drive market participants. Understanding and managing them is the difference between consistent profits and devastating losses. This skill provides techniques to work with these powerful emotions.

## Fear

### Types of Trading Fear

#### 1. Fear of Missing Out (FOMO)

**Trigger:** Price moves up, everyone talking about it
**Thought process:** "I need to get in now or I'll miss the move!"
**Mistake:** Chasing price, poor entries, no [[risk-management]]

**Solution:**
- If you've missed it, you've missed it
- Wait for pullback to better entry
- Remember: Trees don't grow to the sky

#### 2. Fear of Loss

**Trigger:** Price moves against position
**Thought process:** "I can't lose more, I need to exit now!"
**Mistake:** Early exits, cutting profits short, no [[stop-loss-strategies]]

**Solution:**
- Plan exits before entry
- Trust your [[risk-reward-ratio]]
- Use automatic stops

#### 3. Fear of Being Wrong

**Trigger:** Trade goes against you
**Thought process:** "I can't be wrong, it will come back!"
**Mistake:** No stop loss, adding to losers, hoping

**Solution:**
- Accept being wrong is part of trading
- Cut losers quickly
- Being wrong small > Being right big

#### 4. Fear of Pulling Trigger

**Trigger:** Perfect setup appears
**Thought process:** "What if I'm wrong? What if it fails?"
**Mistake:** Not taking trades that meet criteria

**Solution:**
- Define your setup clearly
- Trust your [[trading-routine]]
- Paper trade if fear is paralyzing

### Fear Management Techniques

#### Pre-Trade Visualization

Before entering, visualize:
- Best case (hit target)
- Worst case (hit stop)
- Accept both outcomes before they happen

#### The "What If" Exercise

Ask and answer:
- "What if price hits my stop?" → I lose $X, I'm fine with it
- "What if price hits my target?" → I make $Y, I take profit
- "What if it does nothing?" → I exit at time stop

If you can't accept worst case → Don't take the trade.

#### Small Size Approach

When fear is high:
- Trade smaller than usual
- Build confidence with small wins
- Scale up as comfort increases

## Greed

### Types of Trading Greed

#### 1. Overtrading Greed

**Trigger:** Wanting to maximize activity
**Thought process:** "I need to be in more trades!"
**Mistake:** Poor setups, excessive risk, [[forced-engagement-produces-weak-connections]]

**Solution:**
- Set daily trade limit (e.g., max 3 trades)
- Quality over quantity
- See [[market-psychology]] on activity bias

#### 2. Position Size Greed

**Trigger:** Big win, confidence spike
**Thought process:** "I should have risked more!"
**Mistake:** Increasing position size, breaking [[position-sizing]] rules

**Solution:**
- Keep 1% rule always
- Increase size only after proven consistency
- One big win ≠ sudden skill increase

#### 3. Profit Taking Greed

**Trigger:** Price moves in favor
**Thought process:** "I'll let it run to the moon!"
**Mistake:** Not taking profits when plan says to

**Solution:**
- Have profit targets
- Take partial profits at levels
- Don't be too greedy

#### 4. Leverage Greed

**Trigger:** Wanting to maximize returns
**Thought process:** "More leverage = more profit!"
**Mistake:** Using excessive margin, blowing up account

**Solution:**
- Use minimal leverage initially
- Increase only after proven success
- Remember: Leverage works both ways

### Greed Management Techniques

#### The 24-Hour Rule

After a big win:
- Wait 24 hours before increasing size
- Assess whether it was skill or luck
- If luck → Keep size the same
- If skill → Consider modest increase

#### Profit-Taking Discipline

When price hits target:
- Take partial profits (e.g., 50%)
- Move stop to breakeven on remaining
- Don't get greedy about "more"

#### Win Streak Protocol

After 3+ winning trades in a row:
- Reduce position size by 25%
- Be extra vigilant about [[risk-management]]
- Remember: Overconfidence → Big loss coming

## Market Sentiment Indicators

### Put/Call Ratio

Measures fear/greed in options market:
- **High (>1.0):** Excessive fear → Potential buying opportunity
- **Low (<0.5):** Excessive greed → Caution for long positions

### VIX (Fear Index)

Measures expected volatility:
- **High (>30):** High fear → Contrarians buy
- **Low (<15):** Low fear → Be careful

### Social Media Hype

When everyone's talking about same trade:
- "Everyone knows" → Usually too late
- **Contrarian signal:** Time to be cautious

## Integration with Emotions

### Fear Cycle

```
Setup appears → Fear of missing out → Enter poorly
→ Fear of loss → Exit early → Fear of being wrong → No stop
→ Loss grows → Fear triggers → Revenge trading → More losses
```

**Break the cycle** with [[emotional-control]] techniques.

### Greed Cycle

```
Winning trade → Greed for more → Increase size
→ Overconfidence → Ignore risk → Loss triggers
→ Fear kicks in → Bad decisions → More losses
```

**Break the cycle** by:
- Sticking to [[position-sizing]]
- Taking profits when planned
- Remaining humble after wins

## Quick Self-Check

### Fear Signals

If you're thinking:
- "I can't miss this move!" → FOMO, step back
- "I can't take this loss!" → Poor [[risk-management]]
- "I know it will come back!" → Denial, accept loss

### Greed Signals

If you're thinking:
- "I should have risked more!" → Greed, stay disciplined
- "This will go to the moon!" → Unrealistic, take profits
- "I need more trades today!" → Overtrading, stop

## Daily Practices

### Morning Check-In

Before trading, assess your emotional state:
- Am I calm or anxious?
- Am I patient or rushed?
- Am I realistic or unrealistic?

If any answer is negative → No trading today.

### Evening Review

After trading, ask:
- How did fear/greed affect decisions?
- What triggered these emotions?
- What will I do differently tomorrow?

Track patterns over time.

## Quick Rules

1. **If FOMO hits** → Step away, wait 30 minutes, re-evaluate
2. **If fear of loss** → Check your [[risk-reward-ratio]], trust the plan
3. **If greed hits** → Remember your 1% rule, stay humble
4. **If overtrading** → Set daily limit, stick to it
5. **Always have stops** — Fear and greed both bypass discipline

Fear and greed will never disappear.

You can only learn to work with them.
