# Mean Reversion

## Overview

Mean reversion trades extremes. When price moves too far too fast, it tends to snap back to the mean. This is counter-trend trading — requires skill and discipline.

## Core Principle

**What goes up too fast comes down.**
**What goes down too fast comes up.**

## Key Indicators

- **RSI:** Oversold (<30) or overbought (>70)
- **Bollinger Bands:** Price outside bands
- **Support/Resistance:** Price at key levels
- **Divergence:** Price makes new high, indicator doesn't

## Setup

### 1. Extreme Condition

- Price outside Bollinger Bands
- RSI > 70 or < 30
- Multiple tests of level

### 2. Confirmation Signal

- Price holding at extreme
- Volume drying up on push
- Divergence on indicators

### 3. Entry and Exit

**Entry:** At extreme with confirmation
**Stop:** Beyond extreme (new extreme)
**Target:** Mean or opposite band

**R:R:** 1:1 to 1.5:1 (see [[risk-reward-ratio]])

## Common Mistakes

- Trading too early (no confirmation)
- Holding too long (trend reasserts)
- Overtrading (see [[forced-engagement-produces-weak-connections]])
- Ignoring market context

See [[market-psychology]] for reversion phase behavior.

| Condition | Trade |
|-----------|-------|
| RSI < 30 + Support | Buy |
| RSI > 70 + Resistance | Sell |

| Price above upper band | Sell |

## Wisdom

> "Markets can remain irrational longer than you can remain solvent." — Keynes

Don't fight strong trends.
Wait for extremes.
Take profits at the mean.
