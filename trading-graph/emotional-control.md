# Emotional Control

## Overview

Trading is 20% analysis, 80% psychology. Your system can be perfect, but without emotional control, you'll still lose. This skill provides techniques to master your emotions during trading.

## Pre-Trade Ritual

### The 3-Question Check

Before every trade, ask:
1. **What's my plan?** (Entry, exit, stop loss, size)
2. **Why now?** (Setup quality, not FOMO)
3. **What if I'm wrong?** (Accept the loss before it happens)

If you can't answer all three → No trade.

### Emotional Temperature Check

Rate your emotional state 1-10:
- **1-3 (Too low):** Apathetic, bored → Forced trades
- **4-7 (Optimal):** Calm, focused → Good decisions
- **8-10 (Too high):** Excited, fearful → Overreacting

Only trade in 4-7 range.

## During Trade Techniques

### The "Pause Button"

When you feel:
- "I need to exit now!"
- "I should add more!"
- "This will recover!"

**Take 30 seconds:** Close your eyes, breathe, ask: "Is this my plan or my emotions talking?"

### Detached Observation

Watch the market like you're watching someone else trade:
- "Oh, price is approaching my stop loss"
- "Interesting, support is holding"
- "I wonder what the market will do next"

Remove "my" from your thinking during the trade.

### Breathing Exercise

When stress spikes:
1. Inhale 4 seconds
2. Hold 4 seconds
3. Exhale 4 seconds
4. Hold 4 seconds

Repeat 3-5 times. Resets cortisol response.

## Post-Trade Analysis

### The No-Blame Review

Regardless of outcome, ask:
- Did I follow my [[trading-routine]]?
- Was my [[position-sizing]] correct?
- How did I feel at key decision points?

No self-criticism. Just data collection. See [[market-psychology]] for patterns.

### Emotional Journal

Track emotions, not just trades:
```
Date | Emotion | Trigger | Response | Outcome
```

Over time, you'll see your [[fear-and-greed]] patterns.

## Common Emotional Triggers

### Fear-Based Mistakes

| Emotion | Trigger | Mistake | Solution |
|---------|---------|---------|----------|
| Fear of missing out | Price moves up | Chasing price | Wait for retest |
| Fear of loss | Red candle | Early exit | Trust [[risk-management]] |
| Fear of being wrong | Market moves against | No stop loss | Hard stops mandatory |

### Greed-Based Mistakes

| Emotion | Trigger | Mistake | Solution |
|---------|---------|---------|----------|
| Greed | Winning streak | Over-trading | Daily trade limit |
| Greed | Big win | Increase size | Keep 1% rule |
| Greed | "Easy money" | Ignore risk | See [[forced-engagement-produces-weak-connections]] |

## Building Discipline

### Start Small

When building emotional control:
- Trade smaller size than normal
- Reduce number of trades per day
- Focus on process, not P&L

### The 21-Day Rule

It takes ~21 days to build a habit. Commit to:
1. Follow your [[trading-routine]] every day
2. No deviation, no exceptions
3. Review progress weekly

### Accountability Partner

Share your:
- Trade plan before session
- Results after session
- Emotional struggles

Humans follow through when others are watching.

## Reset Techniques

### When You're Tilted

Signs you're on tilt:
- "I'll just one more trade" (after losses)
- Revenge trading mentality
- Can't think clearly
- Anger at the market

**Reset Protocol:**
1. Stop trading immediately
2. Walk away for 30 minutes minimum
3. Don't look at charts
4. Come back with fresh mind or wait until tomorrow

### When You're Too Excited

Signs:
- "This is my big winner!"
- Can't stop thinking about the trade
- Want to add to position aggressively
- Bragging to friends

**Reset Protocol:**
1. Take partial profits (if it makes sense)
2. Remember: "Trees don't grow to the sky"
3. Read [[market-psychology]] on euphoria
4. Stay humble
