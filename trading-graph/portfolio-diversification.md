# Portfolio Diversification

## Overview

Portfolio diversification spreads risk across multiple assets and strategies. It's [[risk-management]] at the portfolio level. Don't put all your eggs in one basket — unless you enjoy breaking them.

## Core Principle

**Uncorrelated assets = Smoother equity curve = Better sleep**

## Types of Diversification

### 1. Asset Class Diversification

Spread across:
- **Stocks:** Equities from different sectors
- **Bonds:** Fixed income, various maturities
- **Commodities:** Gold, oil, agricultural
- **Currencies:** Forex pairs, uncorrelated
- **Real Estate:** REITs, physical property
- **Crypto:** Bitcoin, Ethereum (if you dare)

### 2. Sector Diversification

Don't be concentrated:
- **Tech:** AAPL, MSFT, GOOGL
- **Finance:** JPM, BAC, GS
- **Healthcare:** JNJ, PFE, UNH
- **Consumer:** AMZN, TSLA, MCD

**Rule:** No single sector > 25% of portfolio

### 3. Geography Diversification

Spread countries:
- **US:** Largest, most liquid
- **Europe:** Developed markets
- **Emerging:** Higher growth, higher risk
- **Asia:** China, Japan, others

### 4. Strategy Diversification

Mix approaches:
- **Trend Following:** Ride big moves
- **Mean Reversion:** Fade extremes
- **Breakout:** Catch breakouts
- **Income:** Dividends, yield

## Correlation Risk

### What Is Correlation?

How assets move together:
- **+1:** Perfect correlation (move same direction)
- **0:** No correlation (independent)
- **-1:** Perfect inverse correlation (opposite direction)

### The Problem with High Correlation

If all your assets are correlated:
- They all go up together ✅
- They all go down together ❌

**Result:** No real diversification.

### Checking Correlation

**Example:**
- AAPL vs MSFT: High correlation (~0.7)
- AAPL vs GLD: Low correlation (~0.1)
- AAPL vs TLT: Negative correlation (~-0.3)

**Solution:** Mix low-correlation assets.

## Position-Level Rules

### 1. Max Position Size

- No single position > 10% of portfolio
- Riskier positions: 3-5% max
- Safer positions: 7-10% max

See [[position-sizing]] for trade-level sizing.

### 2. Sector Concentration

- No single sector > 25% of portfolio
- Correlated sectors count together (Tech + Software = Tech exposure)
- Rebalance if concentration grows

### 3. Risk Parity

Equalize risk contribution:
- Not equal dollar allocation
- Equal volatility contribution

**Example:**
- $1,000 in low-volatility stocks
- $500 in high-volatility stocks
- Both contribute equal risk

## Rebalancing

### Why Rebalance?

Over time, winners grow:
- Winner: +50%, now larger % of portfolio
- Loser: -30%, now smaller % of portfolio
- Portfolio drifts from target allocation

**Result:** Risk profile changes.

### When to Rebalance

- **Time-based:** Quarterly, semi-annually
- **Threshold-based:** Any position deviates >5% from target
- **Hybrid:** Quarterly + if >5% deviation

### How to Rebalance

1. Calculate current allocation
2. Compare to target allocation
3. Sell over-weighted positions
4. Buy under-weighted positions
5. Minimize trading costs (tax, commissions)

## Common Mistakes

### 1. Over-Diversification

Too many positions:
- 50+ individual stocks
- Can't monitor all
- Transaction costs kill
- Index fund better

**Solution:** 10-20 quality positions or use funds.

### 2. False Diversification

Seems diversified, but isn't:
- 10 different tech stocks = still 100% tech
- 5 different ETFs tracking same index
- S&P 500 vs Total Stock Market = same exposure

**Solution:** Check underlying holdings.

### 3. Concentration Risk Creep

Starts diversified:
- Tech winner: 10% → 20% → 30%
- "It's doing great, let it run!"
- Then tech crashes

**Solution:** Rebalance winners too.

## Diversification by Trader Type

### Conservative Trader

- 60% stocks, 30% bonds, 10% cash
- Dividend-paying stocks
- Low-volatility ETFs
- Maximum 3% risk per trade

### Balanced Trader

- 70% stocks, 20% bonds, 10% alternatives
- Growth + value mix
- Sector ETFs
- Maximum 5% risk per trade

### Aggressive Trader

- 80% stocks, 15% alternatives, 5% cash
- Higher beta stocks
- Sector concentration (but limited)
- Maximum 10% risk per trade

## Portfolio Risk Management

### Drawdown Limits

- **Warning:** 15% drawdown → Review strategy
- **Action:** 25% drawdown → Reduce risk, re-evaluate
- **Emergency:** 40% drawdown → Stop trading, regroup

### Correlation Monitoring

Track portfolio correlations quarterly:
- If correlations rising → Diversification failing
- Consider adding low-correlation assets
- May mean market regime change

### Stress Testing

Scenario analysis:
- What if tech drops 30%?
- What if rates rise 2%?
- What if market crashes 20%?

See [[risk-management]] for trade-level risk.

## Wisdom

> "Diversification is protection against ignorance. It makes little sense if you know what you're doing." — Warren Buffett

If you're Warren Buffett: Concentrate.

If you're everyone else: Diversify.

Most traders: Diversify.
